Frequently asked questions

What is GuardianUI?

GuardianUI is an end-to-end testing and monitoring platform for web3 apps.

We automate end-to-end testing of web3 critical paths, monitor your app, and provide real-time alerting and observability to ensure deployed apps create the expected smart contract interactions for users.

What is GuardianUI testing and monitoring for exactly?

We check that your app’s frontend is creating the expected smart contract write transactions. Specifically, we make sure transactions point to the right contracts and approvals give the appropriate access to user funds.

Why is monitoring and securing our frontend important?

Users deserve safe and secure experiences, and there are tons of ways users can get rugged while using your app without you knowing it. Whether it’s bad code accidentally getting pushed to production, supply chain attacks, or middleware attacks - your app’s frontend has significant risk vectors that’s impossible for you and your team to manually monitor. That’s where we come in. With GuardianUI, you can set it and forget it - by continuously monitoring that your frontend is working as expected. If it doesn’t, we’ll immediately notify you of exactly what’s wrong so you can address the issue immediately and keep your users safe.

What are examples of things GuardianUI protects against?

GuardianUI monitors for anything that might cause your frontend to create the wrong smart contract interactions and rug your users. This includes middleware and supply chain attacks that could compromise your frontend and alter its contract interactions, such as DNS attacks, Cloudflare attacks, javascript injections, malicious package injections, package name squatting, malicious minifiers, compromised linters, and more. It also includes incorrect code getting pushed to production.

What are examples of web3 hacks GuardianUI would protect against?

There are countless examples of FE attacks resulting in users getting rugged. Here are a few:

  • BadgerDAO (Cloudflare) - $120M in user funds lost
  • Sushiswap’s Miso (Supply Chain) - $3M in user funds lost
  • Curve Finance (DNS) - $600k in user funds lost
  • Ribbon Finance (DNS) - $500k in user funds lost
  • Celer Protocol (DNS) - $250k in user funds lost
  • Black Wallet (DNS) - $400k in user funds lost
  • Pancake Swap (DNS) - prompted users to enter seed phrase
  • Cream Finance (DNS) - prompted users to enter seed phrase

What end-to-end testing framework does GuardianUI use?

We’ve extended the popular Playwright framework to include an injected wallet for testing interactions between your frontend and your smart contracts.

What blockchains does GuardianUI cover?

Currently, GuardianUI only monitors apps on Ethereum mainnet, but we plan to expand our coverage to all EVM compatible chains. If your project is not on mainnet, we want to hear from you! Please email us and let us know more, so we know which chains to prioritize next!

We already run tests in our development pipeline. Why do we also need GuardianUI?

GuardianUI monitors your live app to make sure it’s safe for users. We continuously check to make sure your app’s live UI is creating the expected smart contract interactions for your users. If a check fails for some reason (e.g. an attack or simply bad code), we alert you immediately with information to address the root issue so you can keep your users safe.

What happens when a check fails?

If a check fails, GuardianUI will immediately send your team an alert via email and/or discord with the exact failure reason. We eliminate the guesswork so you can immediately address the root issue. For more about alerts, please see our docs.

Does the GuardianUI testing framework also test state change?

Currently, our framework mocks transactions and approvals to check that contract interactions are correct but does not submit transactions on chain.

How many tests should we write for our app?

Every app is unique with different security needs. As a general rule, you should consider creating tests for any interaction your frontend has with smart contracts. We are happy to write your tests for you if you’d prefer we do it instead of your team.
